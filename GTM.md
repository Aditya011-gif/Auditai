# Go-To-Market

## Target User

Seed-stage technical founder or engineering manager at a 3–30 person startup already paying for at least two of: Cursor, ChatGPT, Claude, GitHub Copilot, Windsurf, or an OpenAI/Anthropic API account. They are not searching for "AI procurement software." They encounter the tool after seeing a surprise API invoice, a teammate requesting another seat, a vendor repricing announcement, or a founder on X posting their monthly AI stack bill.

The trigger is pain, not search intent. GTM must intercept that pain at the moment it surfaces.

## Channel 1 — X / Twitter (highest intent, lowest friction)

Post a thread showing three real anonymized audit outputs:

> "I audited three AI stacks this week. Here's what I found:
> 
> 1. 4-person seed team: Cursor Business ($160/mo) → Cursor Pro ($80/mo). Admin controls not being used. Saves $80/mo, $960/yr.
> 2. 2-person startup: ChatGPT Team ($60/mo) → ChatGPT Plus ($40/mo). Team features unused. Saves $20/mo.
> 3. 1-person founder: Gemini Ultra ($250/mo) at low usage → Gemini Pro ($20/mo). Saves $230/mo.
> 
> Run your audit free at [URL] — no login, takes 3 minutes."

Target accounts to tag or engage: `@levelsio`, `@swyx`, `@benediktdeicke`, `@patio11`. Reply to threads where founders discuss API costs.

## Channel 2 — Hacker News Show HN

Title: **Show HN: Free AI spend audit for startups — no login, deterministic math, shareable report**

Key facts to lead with: no login, open-source rule logic, report is shareable (link works without account), does not connect to billing APIs.

Expected: 50–200 upvotes if launched on a Tuesday or Wednesday morning US time. Comments will include skeptics asking about pricing accuracy — have the `PRICING_DATA.md` links ready to cite.

## Channel 3 — Subreddits (pain-triggered posts)

Post or comment when founders discuss AI costs in:
- `r/SaaS` — "How much are you spending on AI tools?"
- `r/startups` — vendor pricing discussion threads
- `r/LocalLLaMA` — API cost optimization threads
- `r/ClaudeAI` and `r/GithubCopilot` — plan comparison questions

Do not post promotional links. Engage authentically with cost questions, then offer the audit as a resource.

## Channel 4 — Indie Hackers / Product Hunt

Post a transparent build story: what I built, why the math is deterministic, how many audits have run, what the median savings finding is. These audiences reward honesty about early traction numbers.

## Credex-Specific Angle

Most calculators say "downgrade." Credex can say "you are still going to spend $X this quarter; here is how to source those credits cheaper." This is the only angle that differentiates the tool post-downgrade-recommendation. Surface it prominently in reports where `action === "credits"` is the top recommendation.

## Week-One Funnel Target

| Stage | Target |
|-------|--------|
| Unique visitors | 500 |
| Audit starts | 180 (36%) |
| Audits completed | 90 (50% of starts) |
| Emails captured | 25 (28% of completions) |
| High-savings leads (>$500/mo) | 8 (32% of captured) |
| Consultation calls booked | 3 (38% of high-savings) |

The goal is not broad retention. It is 3–8 qualified founders per week who have demonstrated AI spend pain and intent by completing the audit.
