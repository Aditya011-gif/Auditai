# Economics

Assume a converted Credex customer buys discounted AI credits or subscriptions worth `$12,000` per year at a gross margin of `20%`. That makes first-year gross profit about `$2,400`. If the average customer stays for 18 months, rough LTV is `$3,600` before support costs.

Channel CAC for the MVP can be close to zero in cash but not zero in labor. A strong Show HN post might cost four hours and generate 1,000 visitors. If 20% start an audit, 50% complete it, 25% leave email, and 20% of captured leads qualify, that is 10 qualified leads. If 20% book and 30% buy, that is 0.6 customers, or about `$6,000` CAC equivalent if founder time is valued at `$150/hour`. X founder outreach is more manual: 100 tailored messages might take six hours, produce 20 audits, 8 leads, 4 calls, and 1 customer, or about `$900` labor CAC.

Profitability threshold: with `$3,600` LTV, the tool can tolerate roughly `$1,000` blended CAC and still be attractive. The funnel that makes it work is `audit completed -> consultation booked` at `8–12%`, and `consultation booked -> purchase` at `25–35%`.

For this tool to drive `$1M ARR` in 18 months, Credex needs roughly 83 customers at `$12k ARR`, or fewer customers if average credit purchases are larger. At a 1% visitor-to-customer conversion rate, that requires about 8,300 qualified visitors over 18 months. That is plausible only if the public reports and pricing-change content create repeated distribution, not if every user comes from manual outreach.
