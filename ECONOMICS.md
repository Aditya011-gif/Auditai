# Economics

## Unit Economics Assumptions

| Variable | Value | Basis |
|----------|-------|-------|
| Average annual Credex credit purchase | $12,000 | Credex assignment brief |
| Gross margin on credit brokerage | 20% | Credex assignment brief |
| First-year gross profit per customer | $2,400 | $12,000 × 20% |
| Average customer retention | 18 months | Conservative for a B2B tool in an active vendor market |
| LTV (gross profit × retention factor) | ~$3,600 | $2,400 × 1.5 |

## CAC by Channel

### Show HN / Organic Content (labor CAC)

- 4 hours to write and post a quality Show HN + X thread.
- Expected: 1,000 unique visitors, 200 audit starts, 100 completions, 28 email captures, 9 high-savings leads, 2 calls booked, 0.5 customers expected.
- Founder time valued at $150/hour → labor cost $600.
- Expected customers: ~0.5
- Implied CAC: ~$1,200

### Direct X Founder Outreach (manual)

- 6 hours for 100 tailored replies to cost-complaint threads.
- Expected: 30 audit clicks, 15 completions, 5 leads, 2 calls, 0.6 customers.
- Labor cost: $900.
- Implied CAC: ~$1,500

### Blended target CAC: ≤ $1,000

With LTV of ~$3,600 and a target LTV:CAC ratio of at least 3:1, the tool must stay at or below $1,200 blended CAC to be a viable growth channel for Credex.

## Payback Period

At $2,400 gross profit per customer per year and $1,000 CAC, payback is approximately 5 months. This is acceptable for a B2B SaaS with 18-month average retention.

## Path to $1M ARR

$1,000,000 ARR ÷ $12,000 ACV = **84 active customers**.

| Scenario | Visitors needed | Conversion assumption |
|----------|-----------------|-----------------------|
| 1% visitor-to-customer | 8,400 qualified visitors over 18 months | All stages convert at median rate |
| 0.5% visitor-to-customer | 16,800 qualified visitors | Lower report-to-lead conversion |

8,400 qualified visitors over 18 months is approximately 467 per month. This is achievable only if public report pages and pricing-change content create organic distribution (SEO, shares) — not if every visitor comes from manual outreach.

## Sensitivity: What Must Hold for This to Work

1. **Audit completion rate ≥ 50%** — if the form is too long, cost per completed audit rises sharply.
2. **High-savings report rate ≥ 20%** — fewer than 20% of completed audits finding >$500/mo savings means the tool is attracting the wrong audience (very small or very efficient teams).
3. **Lead capture rate after report view ≥ 20%** — if fewer than 1 in 5 high-savings users leave an email, the funnel breaks at the conversion step.
4. **Credex close rate ≥ 25% of booked calls** — below this, the tool's economics depend on volume that requires paid acquisition.

## Pivot Trigger

If after 200 completed audits the high-savings report rate is below 10%, reprice the targeting: shift from general founders to procurement leads at 20–100 person companies with formal AI tooling budgets, or reposition around renewal-date monitoring rather than one-time savings discovery.
